Explain anemia in simple terms

Anemia is a condition where your body doesn't have enough red blood cells or hemoglobin, which are important for carrying oxygen to different parts of your body. This can cause symptoms like fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath. There are many reasons why someone might develop anemia, including dietary deficiencies, infections, and autoimmune disorders. Treatment options depend on the underlying cause of the anemia, but may include dietary changes, medication, or blood transfusions.

How to cure anemia ?

Anemia can be cured by making dietary changes such as consuming more iron-rich foods, avoiding substances that cause anemia (such as certain medications or exposure to industrial chemicals), taking daily multivitamins, and in some cases, undergoing surgery to repair any damage to the red blood cells.
